From the wreck of The Iron Pelican · 1847
EVIDENCE
14th October · Storm Season
Before the Iron Pelican went down off the rocks of Cape Halvard, her navigator — a seasoned sailor known only as Old Rook — managed to tap out one final morse transmission.

The signal was weak, broken by the crashing waves. But our coast station caught it. Eight letters. Two words.

Old Rook was known to have hidden the treasure chest before the storm struck. The words in his transmission point to where on the island the chest was buried.

The island has three landmarks on the chart. The padlock on the treasure chest bears a single number. Decode the signal. Find the place. Claim the treasure.
⚓ Cape Halvard Coast Station, 1847
